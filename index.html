Welcome to the Energy Factor



Exploring the evolution of energy and its
impact on climate change






Energy has an all-pervasive role in our lives - everything that happens on Earth, in biology, and even human life and culture, depends on “free” energy. Additionally, exploiting energy from any source allows more work and power, promoting more complexity.


“All natural processes and all human actions are, in the most fundamental physical sense, transformations of energy. Civilization’s advances can be seen as a quest for the higher energy use required to produce increased food harvests, to mobilise a greater output and variety of materials, to produce more, and more diverse goods, to enable higher mobility, and to create access to virtually unlimited amounts of information” - Vaclav Smil


Discover the journey of energy through time and its profound impact on our planet and society. From the first sparks of fire to the complexities of the modern world, energy has been the driving force behind progress and challenge. Join us in exploring the pivotal moments in energy evolution, the dilemmas we face today, and the choices that shape our future. Learn how you can make a difference. Together, let's illuminate the path towards a sustainable and energy-conscious world.

Gareth Wyn Jones

Gareth Wyn Jones is a Professor Emeritus of plant biology and bioscience at Bangor University. His field of study focuses on the history of energy and how it has shaped our society, and its implications for climate change. His work is the foundation for this website.
